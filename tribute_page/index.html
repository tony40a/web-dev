Nikola Tesla

The greatest inventor of all time.

Some information about life path of Nikola Tesla:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan Austrian Empire
  • 1870 - Studied engineering and physics at Technical University of Graz and the University of Prague
  • 1882 - Went to work in Paris for the Continental Edison Company
  • 1883 - While on assignment to Strassburg, he constructed, after work hours, his first induction motor
  • 1884 - Immigrated to the United States in 1884 and sold the patent rights to his system of alternating-current dynamos, transformers, and motors to George Westinghouse
  • 1891 - Invented the Tesla coil, an induction coil widely used in radio technology
  • 1898 - Invented the teleautomatic boat guided by remote control
  • 1900 - Settled in New York where he began construction on Long Island of a wireless world broadcasting tower
  • 1917 - Was the recipient of the Edison Medal, the highest honour that the American Institute of Electrical Engineers could bestow
  • 1943 - Dies at the age of 81
